01

About the Event

Revolute is Boston’s first physical AI hackathon, focused on cooking with robot arms.

Teams will spend the weekend building demos that combine perception, control, hardware, and creativity. A camera sees the scene, a model or script makes a decision, and a robot arm tries to do the task.

The goal is to learn, build, debug, help nearby teams, and leave with a working demo, a funny failure, or a better understanding of how physical AI systems come together.

Build something useful, funny, brittle, impressive, or instructive.

02

Theme

Cooking with robot arms.

Projects can involve pouring, plating, sorting ingredients, detecting objects, assisting with prep, interacting with utensils, or any cooking-inspired task that fits the hardware and safety constraints.

03

Who Should Apply

Calling builders, engineers, hackers, researchers, and robotics enthusiasts interested in physical AI.

You do not need prior robotics experience. This weekend is best for people who enjoy learning through ambiguity, reading documentation, asking for help, and making hardware behave.

Apply solo, with friends, or as a preformed team. You can also form a team at the event.

Space is limited by robot stations, venue size, and mentor availability.

FAQ

Can I apply without a team?

Yes. You can apply solo, with friends, or as a preformed team. Teams can also form at the event.

What is the format?

Two days of building, mentoring, food, hardware bring-up, and demos. The final session will be a science-fair style showcase where judges visit each team.

What are the prizes?

TBA. Expect practical maker rewards.

What is Revolute?

Revolute is the name for this recurring Boston physical AI hackathon because “Boston Embodied / Physical AI Robot Hackathon” was too long.
